# Skill: Portfolio Copy & Voice

Editorial rules for every word on the site. Apply before writing any headline, subhead, body copy, label, or CTA. When in doubt, cut.

## The anchor sentence

> Senior Product Manager building enterprise AI and predictive tools for high-stakes industries.

This is the positioning line. Every other sentence on the site either supports it or does not belong.

## Audience

Recruiters and hiring managers at B2B enterprise software companies. Bias toward AI-native and agentic AI PM roles. They will spend 30–90 seconds on the site.

**They are not:** industrial 3D printing specialists. They do not know additive manufacturing, SLS, build chambers, or powder management. Domain language from the manufacturing vertical will cause them to disengage.

**They are:** enterprise software generalists who evaluate PM candidates by pattern-matching to outcomes, scope, and role clarity.

## Translation rules

Always translate industrial/domain language into functional enterprise software language:

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| Additive manufacturing / 3D printing | "manufacturing operations" or omit |
| SLS, FDM, MJF, powder bed | omit entirely |
| Build chamber / build job | "production run" or "manufacturing job" |
| Nesting / packing | omit or "job configuration" |
| Computer vision | "AI-powered" (do not name the technique) |
| Physics simulation engine | "physics-based predictive simulation" |
| Oqton | name is fine; product names are handled by `ip-handling` |
| "SaaS" | omit — these are enterprise products, not SaaS |
| "Platform" (overused) | be specific: "product", "tool", "system" |

## Voice

**Tone:** Direct. Confident without overclaiming. Evidence-first. The work speaks; copy frames it.

**What it sounds like:**
- Lead with the outcome or the problem, not the role
- Use specific numbers when available; never round up or fabricate
- Short sentences over long ones
- Active voice always
- No buzzword stacking ("leverage synergies," "thought leader," "passionate about")
- No hedging ("helped to," "contributed to," "was involved in")

**What it does not sound like:**
- Humble-brag modesty
- Corporate press release
- Resume bullet verbatim
- First-person "I" in headlines (subheads and body copy may use sparingly)

## Section-specific rules

### Hero headline
- Must deliver the anchor positioning line or a punchy variant
- Instrument Serif, large, broken into 2–3 visual lines
- One accent word or phrase in teal — use it to carry the most differentiating claim
- No question marks, no ellipsis, no clever wordplay

### Hero subhead
- 2–3 sentences max
- Lead with a hard metric if possible
- Name both product categories (AI + physics-based) to signal range
- Close with the stakes claim (regulated industries / real consequences)

### Case study intros (Sections 2 & 3)
- Open with the problem, not Michael's role
- Problem framing: what was failing, for whom, at what cost
- Keep domain language out of the first 3 sentences
- Role statement: "Senior PM, full ownership" — no hedging

### Outcomes panels
- Metrics are primary — let numbers lead
- Use amber for the number, neutral text for the descriptor
- Do not editorialize: "98% reduction in active monitoring time" not "dramatically reduced monitoring overhead"
- Only use metrics that appear in the BRIEF or that Michael confirms — never estimate or interpolate

### Section 4 (AI-native PM practice)
- This section can be slightly more personal — it's Michael's actual workflow
- Frame it as present-tense: "this is how PM work gets done now"
- Avoid futurist language ("the future of PM is...") — show, don't predict

### Background (Section 5)
- One framing sentence: QA roots → regulated industries → PM judgment
- No full paragraphs — timeline format
- Titles and companies only; no bullet lists of responsibilities

### Contact (Section 6)
- Minimal copy
- One line that gives permission to reach out
- No "hire me" energy — confident, not needy

## What not to write

- Do not mention "AI-native" without showing what it means (Section 4 is the proof)
- Do not use "builder mindset" as a phrase — show it through the portfolio itself
- Do not reference the portfolio site itself ("this site was built with Claude Code") in body copy — that belongs in meta or a footer note
- Do not write about Michael's passion for anything
- Do not use em dashes for decoration — only for genuine parenthetical clarity
- Do not use "leverage" as a verb
