At full capacity, Knauf designs and prints up to 180 tools per year. Every print job uses 3DXpert Mechanical Simulation to automatically compensate for process-induced deformations.

The Problem

Achieving high geometrical accuracy in metal additive manufacturing is challenging, driven by two distinct deformation sources:

Micro-Weld Stresses (Inherent Strain)

Melting and rapid solidification of each laser track introduces localized stress, leading to overall tension and displacement across the part.

Global Thermal Effects

Cumulative heat input causes the entire part to undergo repeated heating and cooling cycles, leading to macroscopic shrinkage and dimensional deviations beyond what inherent strain models alone can predict.

To compensate with the existing uncoupled simulation, long cooling times or a high number of support structures are typically required — often not economically viable for serial production at 180 tools per year.

The New Module: Thermo-Mechanical Simulation

The new coupled thermo-mechanical simulation combines inherent strain analysis with thermal simulation to fully incorporate both micro-weld stresses and global thermal effects. The demonstrator was printed in 316L, a component approximately 100mm × 140mm × 100mm, with a simulated heat-up of up to 470°C.

Both specimens were printed in a single print job without previous trials or adaptation of simulation parameters — and without countermeasures like increased layer time or additional supports.
