Expected Questions

1 "Walk me through how you built the AI monitoring platform at Oqton from scratch."
Frame: Problem → approach → iteration → results. Start with the pain (manual review of production data), your 0→1 process, the 5-customer rollout, and the 98%/90% numbers. Emphasize customer discovery and how you validated the model's decision engine.
2 "How do you decide what gets AI and what doesn't?"
Frame: High-volume repetitive tasks first, where errors have low cost. AI for augmentation before automation. Manual review for edge cases. At Oqton, we targeted the 80% of cases the model could confidently handle.
3 "How would you improve SysAid Copilot?"
Frame: Start by understanding current usage data. If I had to guess without data: (1) make Copilot proactive — suggest actions before users ask, (2) tighten integration with Agentic Service Management so Copilot can trigger agents, (3) improve the feedback loop so Copilot learns from agent corrections.
4 "How do you measure AI product success beyond accuracy?"
Frame: Time saved per IT agent, reduction in mean-time-to-resolution (MTTR), CSAT scores, adoption rate (% of eligible tickets handled by AI), escalation rate (how often AI-touched tickets get escalated to humans), and net promoter score from IT teams.
5 "How is AI Product Management different from traditional PM?"
Frame: AI PM needs to manage model uncertainty — you're shipping probabilistic systems, not deterministic ones. More investment in evaluation/data quality. Closer collaboration with research/ML teams. User trust is harder to earn and easier to lose. You measure in ranges not exact numbers.
6 "Why SysAid? Why AI for IT service management?"
Frame: ITSM is a massive market dominated by legacy players (ServiceNow). AI-native approach is a genuine disruptor. SysAid's focus on making IT teams' work better — not replacing them — resonates with my experience building AI that augments human work. Also: Israeli company, AI-first culture, hybrid in Israel.
